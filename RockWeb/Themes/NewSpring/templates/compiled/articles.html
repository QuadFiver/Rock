How and why should I repent

Seven-year-old Jimmy snatches a toy car from 5-year-old Joey. Joey pinches him. Mom demands apologies and separates them. Jimmy and Joey both mutter, “Sorry,” and grumble as they stomp to their rooms. The next day, the same scenario plays out with blocks. Instinctively, we know neither Jimmy nor Joey is genuinely sorry. Neither their hearts nor their behavior has changed.
